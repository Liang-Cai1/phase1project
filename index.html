Matthew Perry

From Friends star to addiction advocate

"There's nothing better than a world where everybody's just trying to make each other laugh."

Here is his story:

  • Birth: Born in Williamstown, Massachusetts
  • Early Life: Early life was spend in Ottawa, Canada, raised by his mother. His parents were divorced and he had 5 half-siblings, 4 maternal and 1 paternal. His siblings would clap and cheer him on for his performances, paving the way for his future.
  • Early Roles: Matthew was first credited as a child star in his minor role in "240-Robert" in 1979.
  • Early Roles: Not necessarily the News (1983)
  • Early Roles: Charles in Charge (1985)
  • Early Roles: Silver Spoons (1986)
  • Early Roles: Second Chance (1987-1988)
  • Early Roles: Film debut: A Night in the Life of Jimmy Reardon (1988)
  • Early Roles: Growing Pains (1989)
  • Early Roles: Sydney (1990)
  • Early Roles: Beverly Hills, 90210 (1991)
  • Early Roles: Home Free (1993)
  • Breakthrough: At age 24, as youngest cast member, he was casted as Chandler Bing.
  • Breakthrough: By 2002, the whole cast of Friends was making $1M per episode.
  • Awards: Earned an emmy nomination in 2002 for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series.
  • Later Work: Too many to list.
  • Health and Addiction: He started at very young age of 14 and was already an alcoholic.
  • Early Roles: He got addicted to Vicodin after a jet-ski accident in 1997.
  • Early Roles: During later seasons of Friends, he was not sober on set; either drunk or high.
  • Early Roles: While hospitalized, his colon burst from his substance abuse in 2018.
  • Early Roles: He faked pain to obtain more opioids and ketamine infusion therapy in rehab in 2020.
  • Early Roles: He succumbed to his addiction in 2023; overdosing on ketamine in his own home.
  • Legacy: His book "Friends, Lovers, and the Big Terrible Thing" documented his life long struggle with addiction to raise awareness.
